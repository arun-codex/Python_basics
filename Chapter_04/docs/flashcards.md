# 🧠 Active Recall Flashcards

Use these for your 5-Minute Revision. Cover the answer, read the question, and force yourself to recall the answer before checking.

---

<details>
<summary><b>Question:</b> Which one is mutable? Lists or Tuples?</summary>
<br>
<b>Answer:</b> Lists are Mutable (can be changed). Tuples are Immutable.
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Question:</b> What is the syntax for creating an empty list and an empty tuple?</summary>
<br>
<b>Answer:</b> List: `[]`. Tuple: `()`
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Question:</b> What is wrong with this code? `t = (10)`</summary>
<br>
<b>Answer:</b> It creates an integer, not a tuple. A single-item tuple requires a comma: `t = (10,)`.
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Question:</b> What is the difference between `.append()` and `.insert()`?</summary>
<br>
<b>Answer:</b> `.append(x)` always adds `x` to the very end of the list. `.insert(i, x)` adds `x` at the specific index `i`.
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Question:</b> Can a list contain a mix of strings, integers, and booleans?</summary>
<br>
<b>Answer:</b> Yes. Python lists are heterogeneous.
</details>
